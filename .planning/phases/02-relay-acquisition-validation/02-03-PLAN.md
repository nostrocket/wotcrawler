<threat_model>
## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| relay → crawler (transport) | The relay controls connection lifecycle, result-set size, EOSE timing, and relay notices — all adversary-influenced availability surface. |
| relay NIP-11 doc → limit cache | A relay can advertise hostile/absent limits in its NIP-11 document. |
| crawler → relay (politeness) | Outbound REQ rate must stay polite or the crawler is banned (availability of the data source). |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-02-09 | Denial of Service (availability) | src/relay/mod.rs reconnect | mitigate | Exponential backoff + jitter on reconnect (per 02-SPIKES RELAY-01 decision) prevents synchronized reconnect storms / thundering herd after a shared outage. Task 1. |
| T-02-10 | Denial of Service (availability) | src/relay/rate_limit.rs | mitigate | Per-relay governor GCRA limiter caps outbound REQ rate; `rate-limited` notice triggers per-relay backoff; `blocked`/`restricted` stops traffic to that relay. Keeps the crawler from being IP-banned. Task 1. |
| T-02-11 | Tampering / completeness loss | src/relay/fetch.rs | mitigate | Never trust EOSE as completeness; count-vs-max_limit page-back ensures a relay silently capping at max_limit cannot create a permanent hole in the reachable graph. Task 3. |
| T-02-12 | Denial of Service (silent stall) | src/relay/fetch.rs | mitigate | Explicit per-fetch `timeout`; a timed-out window is treated as requeue, not completion, so a silently-dropped socket cannot stall the crawl indefinitely. Task 3. |
| T-02-13 | Tampering | src/relay/nip11.rs | accept | A relay advertising a hostile NIP-11 doc only affects its own pagination cap; clamped to sane defaults and bounded by the configurable requested limit. Low blast radius (one relay). |
</threat_model>

<objective>
Close BLOCKER 1 (CR-03 residual, RELAY-03): the boundary-second completeness
stall in `src/relay/fetch.rs`. The inclusive page-back (`until = oldest`, not
`oldest - 1`) is correct, but the zero-new-id termination guard
(`paginate_chunk`, ~lines 131-133) cannot distinguish "boundary second genuinely
exhausted" from "a deterministic newest-first relay serving the SAME cap-sized
prefix for a pinned `until=T` while more events remain at that second." With
`cap = 2` and three events `A(T)`, `B(T)`, `C(T)` sharing the oldest second, the
loop pins `until=T`, watches `new_ids` decay to 0, breaks, and silently loses
`C(T)`.

Purpose: restore the RELAY-03 completeness guarantee at the cap boundary against
real deterministic relay behavior — never silently complete a truncated window;
surface the unresolvable stall as a requeue Err so the caller retries rather than
recording a partial follow list.

Output: stall detection in `paginate_chunk` (tracking `prev_until` across loop
iterations), plus a new deterministic-relay test in `tests/pagination.rs` that
FAILS before the fix (silent truncated Ok) and PASSES after (Err surfaced).
</objective>
